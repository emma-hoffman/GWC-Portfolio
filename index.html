BEE EXTINCTION

Bee extinction is a large problem in the world today. In January of 2016, for the first time in history the honey bee was declared endangered in the United States. Can you imagine that? Something that we consider a nuissance because of its abundance is actually endangered. Bees although often times we are running away from them in fear are a significant part of our ecosystem. Not only do they help reduce global warming, but they pollinate plants that produce crops and flowers. THE BEE PROBLEM IS REAL AND WE NEED TO ACT NOW! Don't BEE a Hater, Save the Pollinators!

BEE SAVING ORGANIZATIONS

The Honey Bee Conservancy
Sponsor-A-Hive

With the Conservancy's Sponsor-A-Hive program, you can donate money to the foundation in order to maintain the well being of a hive in the comfort of your own backyard. In this setting, you are being encouraged to embrace an individual passion for the cause. It's like owning your own pet which means there is a lengthy application process and a lot of responsibility. But think about the good you can do for our future!

Donate your time Today!
Castlemaine Bee Sanctuary
Adopt-A-Hive

With the Castlemaine Bee Sanctuary, you can adopt a beehive without the responsibility of tending to the bees. This Sponsor-a-Hive program takes a small weekly fee from you and then takes care of the hives in their own sanctuaries with professional beekeepers. In return you are amounted a share of the honey made as a reward for your donation and aid to the cause.

Planet Bee Organization
Planet Bee Summer Camp

Have a sweet summer with the bees at the Planet Bee Summer Camp in San Francisco, California. For $400 a week you can learn more about the endangered population as well as interact with bees on a more personal level. For those looking for experience, you can also apply to become a CIT for an equally beneficial experience!

They offer volunteer and internship opportunities!
